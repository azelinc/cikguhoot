LEARNING OBJECTIVES
-------------------
- Solve word problems involving fractions, decimals, and percentages
- Apply problem-solving steps: Understand, Plan, Solve, Check
- Find the value of a fraction of a quantity

KEY CONCEPTS
------------

1. SOLVING FRACTION PROBLEMS

When finding a fraction of a quantity:
- Divide the total quantity by the denominator.
- Multiply the result by the numerator.

Example: 3/7 of 140
  140 ÷ 7 = 20
  20 × 3 = 60

2. SOLVING DECIMAL PROBLEMS

When solving decimal word problems:
- Identify the operation needed (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division).
- Align decimal points for addition/subtraction.
- Place decimal point correctly for multiplication/division.

3. SOLVING PERCENTAGE PROBLEMS

To find a percentage of a quantity:
- Convert the percentage to a fraction.
- Multiply by the quantity.

Example: 25% of 280 = 25/100 × 280 = 1/4 × 280 = 70

WORKED EXAMPLES
---------------

Example 1: Adding Fractions (Page 105 - Solve the Problems)
Santesh, Swee Lan, and Rokiah collected recycled materials.
Week 1: 2½ kg | Week 2: 4 kg | Week 3: 3⅕ kg
What is the total mass of the collected materials?

Answer:
Convert to fractions with the same denominator:
2½ = 5/2 = 25/10
4 = 40/10
3⅕ = 16/5 = 32/10

Total = 25/10 + 40/10 + 32/10 = 97/10 = 9⁷⁄₁₀ kg

The total mass of the collected materials is 9⁷⁄₁₀ kg.

Example 2: Fraction of a quantity (Page 106)
The total number of Science Club members is 140.
3/7 of the members are Year 4 pupils.
1/2 of the members are Year 5 pupils.
Calculate the difference between the number of Year 4 and Year 5 pupils.

Answer:
Year 4 pupils: 3/7 of 140 = 140 ÷ 7 × 3 = 20 × 3 = 60
Year 5 pupils: 1/2 of 140 = 140 ÷ 2 = 70
Difference: 70 - 60 = 10

The difference between the number of Year 4 and Year 5 pupils is 10.

Example 3: Decimal addition and subtraction (Page 107)
Three balloon cars:
- Newton: 3.1 m
- Pythagoras: 1.15 m more than Newton car
- Einstein: 2.15 m

What is the difference in distance covered by Pythagoras car and Einstein car?

Answer:
Pythagoras distance: 3.1 m + 1.15 m = 4.25 m
Difference: 4.25 m - 2.15 m = 2.10 m

The difference is 2.1 m.

Number sentence: 3.1 + 1.15 - 2.15 = 2.1

Example 5: Percentage of money (Page 108)
The Mathematics Club received RM280. RM70 was used.
Calculate the percentage of money spent.

Answer:
Percentage = 70/280 × 100% = 25%
The percentage of money spent was 25%.

Example 6: Comparing percentages (Page 109)
Kelvin gets 48 out of 60 marks in Environmental Quiz.
Dina gets 24 out of 32 marks in Road Safety Quiz.
Prem Singh gets 81 out of 90 marks in Flora and Fauna Quiz.
Who has the highest percentage of marks?

Answer:
Kelvin: 48/60 × 100% = 80%
Dina: 24/32 × 100% = 75%
Prem Singh: 81/90 × 100% = 90%

Prem Singh has the highest percentage (90%).

KEY VOCABULARY
--------------
- Fraction of a quantity: Finding a part of a total (e.g., 3/7 of 140)
- Percentage: A number out of 100, shown with the % symbol
- Sum: The result of addition
- Difference: The result of subtraction
- Product: The result of multiplication
- Quotient: The result of division
- Simplify: To reduce a fraction to its simplest form
