LEARNING OBJECTIVES
-------------------
- Round off numbers to the nearest ten, hundred, thousand, and ten thousand
- Apply rounding off in daily life situations

KEY CONCEPTS
------------

1. WHAT IS ROUNDING OFF?

Rounding off means changing a number to a nearby number that is easier to 
work with. The rounded number is close to the original but ends with zeros.

2. RULES FOR ROUNDING OFF

When rounding off to a certain place value, look at the digit to its right:

a) Rounding to the nearest ten thousand:
   - Look at the thousands digit.
   - If the thousands digit is 5, 6, 7, 8, or 9 → ADD 1 to the ten thousands digit.
   - If the thousands digit is 0, 1, 2, 3, or 4 → keep the ten thousands digit the same.
   - Replace all digits to the right with 0.

b) Rounding to the nearest thousand:
   - Look at the hundreds digit.
   - Apply the same 5-9 (round up) or 0-4 (keep same) rule.
   - Replace all digits to the right with 0.

c) The middle value is rounded off to the larger ten, hundred, thousand, 
   and ten thousand.

3. WHY DO WE ROUND OFF NUMBERS?

Rounding off helps us:
- Estimate answers quickly
- Manage budgets and avoid wastage
- Make numbers easier to understand and talk about

WORKED EXAMPLES
---------------

Example 1: Round off 12,295 to the nearest thousand.

Answer:
12,295 is between 12,000 and 13,000.
Look at the hundreds digit: 2 (which is less than 5).
So we keep the thousands digit the same.
12,295 rounded off to the nearest thousand is 12,000.

Example 2: Round off 38,704 to the nearest ten thousand.

Answer:
38,704 is between 30,000 and 40,000.
Look at the thousands digit: 8 (which is more than 5).
So we add 1 to the ten thousands digit: 3 becomes 4.
38,704 rounded off to the nearest ten thousand is 40,000.

Method 1: Number line
38,704 is nearer to 40,000 than to 30,000.

Method 2: Digit rule
If the thousands digit is 5, 6, 7, 8, or 9, add 1 to the ten thousands value.
Replace digits 8, 7, 0, and 4 with 0.
38,704 → 40,000

Example 3: Round off 19,500 ml to the nearest thousand ml.

Answer:
19,500 ml is between 19,000 ml and 20,000 ml.
19,500 ml is exactly in the middle.
The middle value is rounded off to the larger value.
19,500 ml becomes 20,000 ml when rounded off to the nearest thousand ml.

Example 4: Round off RM63,988 to the nearest ten thousand ringgit.

Answer:
RM63,988 is between RM60,000 and RM70,000.
63,988 is nearer to RM60,000.
RM63,988 rounded off to the nearest ten thousand ringgit is RM60,000.

PRACTICE QUESTIONS
------------------

1. Round off RM85,090 to the nearest thousand ringgit.

2. The number of readers of four storybooks:
   44,900 ; 48,720 ; 54,200 ; 58,600
   Which storybooks have 50,000 readers when rounded off to the nearest ten thousand?
   (Hint: Numbers from 45,000 to 54,999 become 50,000)

3. Besides 44,900, what other numbers become 40,000 when rounded off to the nearest ten thousand?

4. Choose the correct answers:
   a) Which of (68,790 ; 68,565 ; 68,489) becomes 69,000 when rounded off to the nearest thousand?
   b) Which of (72,103 ; 76,092 ; 85,520) becomes 80,000 when rounded off to the nearest ten thousand?

5. Round off RM83,609 to the nearest ten thousand ringgit.

6. Round off the following numbers to the nearest hundred, nearest thousand, and nearest ten thousand:
   a) 14,278   b) 46,195   c) 62,045   d) 79,638   e) 95,307

7. State three numbers which become 50,000 when rounded off to the nearest ten thousand.

Mind Teaser:
A number becomes 30,000 when rounded off to the nearest ten thousand, thousand, 
hundred, and ten. What are the thousands digits of the number?

KEY VOCABULARY
--------------
- Round off: To change a number to a nearby number that ends with zeros
- Nearest ten: The multiple of 10 that is closest to the number
- Nearest hundred: The multiple of 100 that is closest to the number
- Nearest thousand: The multiple of 1,000 that is closest to the number
- Nearest ten thousand: The multiple of 10,000 that is closest to the number
