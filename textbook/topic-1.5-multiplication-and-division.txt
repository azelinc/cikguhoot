LEARNING OBJECTIVES
-------------------
- Solve mixed operations involving multiplication and division
- Use elimination methods to simplify calculations

KEY CONCEPTS
------------

1. MULTIPLICATION AND DIVISION TOGETHER

When a problem has both multiplication and division:
- Follow the order from left to right.
- OR, you can sometimes eliminate (cancel out) common factors to simplify.

2. ELIMINATION METHOD

If the number you are multiplying by and the number you are dividing by 
share a common factor, you can simplify first.

Example: 8 × 6 ÷ 4
Method 1: Multiply first, then divide
  8 × 6 = 48
  48 ÷ 4 = 12

Method 2: Eliminate first
  8 × 6 ÷ 4 = 2 × 6 = 12
  (Because 8 ÷ 4 = 2)

WORKED EXAMPLES
---------------

Example 1: Multiply 630 by 15. Divide the product by 30.

Answer:
630 × 15 ÷ 30 =
  630 × 15 = 9,450
  9,450 ÷ 30 = 315

OR eliminate first:
  630 ÷ 30 = 21
  21 × 15 = 315

So 630 × 15 ÷ 30 = 315.

Example 2: 21 × 3,059 ÷ 7

Answer:
Method 1: Multiply first, then divide.
  21 × 3,059 = 64,239
  64,239 ÷ 7 = 9,177

Method 2: Eliminate first (21 ÷ 7 = 3).
  21 × 3,059 ÷ 7 = 3 × 3,059 = 9,177

So 21 × 3,059 ÷ 7 = 9,177.

Example 3: 14,100 ÷ 50 × 3

A factory produces 14,100 pairs of shoes. They are equally distributed to 
50 shoe shops. 3 of the shops are Din's Shoe Shop. How many pairs of shoes 
are distributed to Din's Shoe Shop?

Answer:
14,100 ÷ 50 = 282
282 × 3 = 846
846 pairs of shoes are distributed to Din's Shoe Shop.

So 14,100 ÷ 50 × 3 = 846.

Example 4: Divide 89,000 by 1,000. Then multiply the quotient by 67.

Answer:
89,000 ÷ 1,000 = 89
89 × 67 = 5,963
So 89,000 ÷ 1,000 × 67 = 5,963.

Example 5: 42,000 ÷ 42 × 100

Answer:
42,000 ÷ 42 = 1,000
1,000 × 100 = 100,000
So 42,000 ÷ 42 × 100 = 100,000.

KEY VOCABULARY
--------------
- Multiply: To add a number to itself a certain number of times
- Divide: To split into equal parts or groups
- Product: The result of multiplication
- Quotient: The result of division
- Eliminate: Cancel out common factors to simplify calculations
- Mixed operations: Problems that involve more than one type of operation
