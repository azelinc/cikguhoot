LEARNING OBJECTIVES
-------------------
- Multiply decimals by whole numbers
- Multiply decimals by 10, 100, and 1,000
- Place the decimal point correctly in the answer

KEY CONCEPTS
------------

1. MULTIPLYING DECIMALS BY WHOLE NUMBERS

Multiply the same way as whole numbers.
Then place the decimal point in the answer.
The number of decimal places in the answer equals the number of decimal 
places in the decimal number you started with.

2. MULTIPLYING DECIMALS BY 10, 100, AND 1,000

To multiply by 10: Shift the decimal point 1 place to the RIGHT.
To multiply by 100: Shift the decimal point 2 places to the RIGHT.
To multiply by 1,000: Shift the decimal point 3 places to the RIGHT.

This is because the value of the product increases.

WORKED EXAMPLES
---------------

Example 1: 3 × 1.7 ℓ

Answer:
  1. 7
×   3
  5. 1

3 × 1.7 ℓ = 5.1 ℓ
(1.7 has 1 decimal place, so the answer has 1 decimal place)

The total volume of water in 3 similar bottles is 5.1 ℓ.

Example 2: 4 × 2.54 cm

Answer:
  2. 5 4
×     4
  1 0. 1 6

4 × 2.54 cm = 10.16 cm
(2.54 has 2 decimal places, so the answer has 2 decimal places)

The total length of 4 similar rubbers is 10.16 cm.

Example 3: 73.082 × 6

Answer:
  7 3. 0 8 2
×         6
  4 3 8. 4 9 2

73.082 × 6 = 438.492
(73.082 has 3 decimal places, so the answer has 3 decimal places)

Example 4: 10 × 1.25 cm

Answer:
  1. 2 5
× 1 0
  1 2. 5 0

10 × 1.25 cm = 12.5 cm
(Ignore the 0 after number 5: 12.50 = 12.5)

The length of hair growth in the 10th month is 12.5 cm.

Quick way: To multiply by 10, shift the decimal point 1 place to the right.
1.25 × 10 = 12.5

Example 5: 100 × 7.342

Answer:
7.342 × 100 = 734.2
(Shift the decimal point 2 places to the right)

Example 6: 10 × 0.956 = 9.56
         100 × 0.956 = 95.6
       1,000 × 0.956 = 956
(Shift the decimal point 1, 2, or 3 places to the right)

KEY VOCABULARY
--------------
- Multiply: To add a number to itself a certain number of times
- Product: The result of multiplication
- Decimal point: The dot that separates whole numbers from decimal parts
- Decimal places: The number of digits after the decimal point
- Shift: To move the decimal point to the right (for multiplication by 10, 100, 1,000)
