LEARNING OBJECTIVES
-------------------
- Estimate quantities in daily life situations
- Use reference sets to make reasonable estimations

KEY CONCEPTS
------------

1. WHAT IS ESTIMATION?

Estimation is making a careful guess about the amount or quantity of something.
We estimate when we don't need an exact number, just a close guess.

Estimation is very important in daily life such as:
- Estimating the amount of ingredients before cooking
- Estimating expenses before spending

2. HOW TO ESTIMATE

To estimate, we can:
- Compare with a known reference amount
- Use familiar quantities as a guide
- Think about whether the actual amount is more or less than the reference

WORKED EXAMPLES
---------------

Example 1: Estimating Quantities
A jar (Jar A) contains 5,500 water balls. Jar B is bigger than Jar A.
Three pupils gave their estimates:
- Hazel: "About 10,000 balls"
- Vinod: "Nearly 10,000 balls"
- Anis: "20,000 balls"

Whose estimation is the most accurate? Why?

Answer:
The number of water balls in Jar B must be less than 20,000.
Since Jar B is bigger than Jar A (which has 5,500), the answer should be more than 5,500.
About 10,000 or nearly 10,000 seems reasonable.
Anis's estimate of 20,000 is too high.

Example 2: Estimating Volume
Pool R has 4,000 litres of water.
The height of pool S is almost 4 times the height of pool R.
The height of pool T is about double the height of pool R.

Estimate the volume of water in pools S and T.

Answer:
Pool S: 4 × 4,000 litres = almost 16,000 litres
Pool T: 2 × 4,000 litres = about 8,000 litres

PRACTICE QUESTIONS
------------------

1. Estimate the number of beads in containers A and B.
   Container A has 17,000 beads.

2. Estimate the volume of water in bottle C.
   A reference of 10,000 ml is given.

3. Try to guess the number of water balls in jar B when jar A contains 5,500 balls.

KEY VOCABULARY
--------------
- Estimation: A careful guess about quantity or amount
- Estimate (verb): To make a careful guess
- Quantity: An amount or number of something
- Reference set: A known amount used to compare and estimate other amounts
